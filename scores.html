Scoring system

Tetris has many competitions still to this day. The most common version used is 1989's NES tetris. Tetris is primarily a high score based game which lends itself well to competitive play. Players drop tetrominoes onto the matrix to clear lines resulting in points, the aim of the game is to gain as many points as you can before 'topping out' the matrix. Players go head to head both playing individual games to see who will get the highest score.

The scoring system for NES is as follows:

level points for 1 line points for 2 lines points for 3 lines points for 4 lines
0 40 100 300 1200
1 80 200 600 2400
2 120 300 900 3600
... ... ... ... ...
9 400 1000 3000 12000
n 40*(n+1) 100*(n+1) 300*(n+1) 1200*(n+1)
